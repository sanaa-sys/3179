World Bank Health Indicators Data 2019

The World Bank is one of the world's largest producers of data for research. Along with UN, it has developed the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) which aim to assess a country's progress in ending poverty. The indicators measure the country's progress towards achieving the SDGs and we will be looking at health indicators worldwide.

Population

As of 2019, the world population is approx. 7 billion and is predicted to rise. This rise in population is attributed to many factors such as medical advancements and rise in agricultural productivity which has decreased mortality rate and famine all over the world.

Life Expentancy and Fertility Rate

It is interesting to note that life expectancy increases with a decrease in fertility rate. This can be due to the fact that the energetic and metabolic costs from reproduction weakens the human body which increases fatality rate. Also, developed countries have higher life expentancy and lower fertility rate than developing countries. This can be due to increased literacy rate which involves women knowning and using more about contraceptives and other birth control options. Also women in these countries may have less societal burden to have children and can devote more time to themselves.
